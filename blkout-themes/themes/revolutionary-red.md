# Revolutionary Red Theme

**Tagline**: Bold Action • Unapologetic Power • Revolutionary Energy

---

## Theme Identity

**Purpose**: Primary BLKOUT brand theme for action-oriented, revolutionary content that demands attention and inspires movement.

**When to Use**:
- Campaign launches
- Calls to action
- Activist content
- Urgent community announcements
- Revolutionary manifestos
- Power-building materials

**Emotional Resonance**: Bold, urgent, powerful, unapologetic, revolutionary

---

## Color Specification

### Primary Color
**BLKOUT Red**: `#D4261A`
- RGB: (212, 38, 26)
- HSL: (3°, 78%, 47%)
- Usage: Headers, CTAs, brand emphasis, revolutionary statements

### Secondary Color
**Deep Forest**: `#264653`
- RGB: (38, 70, 83)
- HSL: (197°, 37%, 24%)
- Usage: Body text, grounding elements, stability

### Accent Color
**Warm Gold**: `#F4A261`
- RGB: (244, 162, 97)
- HSL: (27°, 87%, 67%)
- Usage: Highlights, warmth, community connection

### Background Colors
**Primary Background**: `#FFFFFF` (White)
- Clean, accessible, maximum contrast

**Alt Background**: `#FFF5F5` (Off-white with red tint)
- Subtle warmth, branded feel

**Dark Variant**: `#1A1A1A` with white text
- For dark mode or dramatic emphasis


## Accessibility

### Contrast Ratios (WCAG AA)
- BLKOUT Red on White: 5.9:1 ✅
- Deep Forest on White: 10.7:1 ✅
- Warm Gold on White: 3.2:1 (large text only)
- White on BLKOUT Red: 5.9:1 ✅
- White on Deep Forest: 10.7:1 ✅

### Best Practices
- Use BLKOUT Red for large text (24px+) or bold CTAs
- Deep Forest for all body text
- Warm Gold as accent only, not for critical text
- Always test with contrast checkers
- Provide text alternatives for color-coded information

## Do's and Don'ts

### ✅ DO
- Use BLKOUT Red boldly for maximum impact
- Pair with Deep Forest for grounding
- Include Warm Gold accents for community warmth
- Maintain high contrast for accessibility
- Embrace revolutionary energy in design

### ❌ DON'T
- Dilute the red with pastels
- Use too many colors (stick to 3-color palette)
- Place red text on red backgrounds
- Make CTAs subtle or hidden
- Tone down the revolutionary aesthetic
