# BLKOUT Brand Guidelines

## Brand Identity

**BLKOUT** is a community-owned liberation platform for and by Black queer men in the UK, built on principles of cooperative ownership, democratic governance, and collective liberation.

**Core Mission**: Liberation technology that centers Black queer men's experiences in the UK, challenges extractive systems, and builds community power through digital sovereignty and cooperative ownership.

**Community**: Black queer men in the United Kingdom - our lived experiences, our needs, our power, our platform.

---

## Brand Values

### For and By Black Queer Men
- Centering Black queer men's lived experiences in the UK
- Platform built by the community it serves
- Black queer masculine expression and joy
- UK-specific needs and cultural context

### Liberation First
- Collective liberation through technology
- Abolition-minded design
- Community data sovereignty
- Anti-capitalist principles
- Challenging racism, homophobia, and extractive systems

### Democratic Governance
- Cooperative ownership model
- One member, one vote
- Transparent decision-making
- Community-driven development
- Accountable to Black queer men in the UK

### Solidarity & Allyship
- Intersectional solidarity with all liberation movements
- Trans solidarity and support
- Cross-community coalition building
- Global Black queer connections
- Disability justice

## Messaging Guidelines

### Voice & Tone

**Voice**: Revolutionary, warm, direct, unapologetic, rooted in Black queer men's UK experiences
**Tone Adapts By Context**:
- **Liberation content**: Bold, defiant, visionary, UK-grounded
- **Community updates**: Warm, brotherly, celebratory
- **Technical docs**: Clear, accessible, empowering
- **Governance**: Transparent, democratic, accountable to members

### Key Phrases

**Use These**:
- "For and by Black queer men in the UK"
- "Community-owned by Black queer men"
- "Liberation technology"
- "Democratic governance"
- "Black queer men's platform"
- "Cooperative ownership"
- "Data sovereignty"
- "Our community, our platform, our power"

**Avoid These**:
- "Users" (say "members" or "brothers")
- "Consumers" (say "community members")
- "Monetize" (say "sustain" or "support")
- "Growth hacking" (say "community building")
- Generic tech jargon
- Erasing the specificity of Black queer men's experiences

### Content Principles

1. **Center Black Queer Men**: Black queer men's lived experiences in the UK come first, always
2. **Name Power**: Call out racism, homophobia, and extractive systems directly
3. **Offer Hope**: Pair critique with vision for liberation and collective power
4. **Honor Complexity**: Avoid oversimplification of Black queer men's lives and experiences
5. **Build Power**: Every piece should build collective capacity among Black queer men
6. **UK Context**: Acknowledge UK-specific realities, culture, and political landscape
7. **Solidarity Not Charity**: Build with allied communities, not speak for them

## Logo Usage

**Primary Logo**: "BLKOUT" in Poppins Bold
- Color: Primary Red `#D4261A` on light backgrounds
- Color: White `#FFFFFF` on dark/colored backgrounds
- Minimum size: 120px width
- Clear space: 20px all sides

**Logo Variations**:
- Full color on white
- White on Primary Red
- White on Deep Forest
- Monochrome (black/white only) for print

**Do Not**:
- Stretch or distort
- Add effects (shadows, outlines, glows)
- Place on busy backgrounds without sufficient contrast
- Use off-brand colors

---

## Accessibility Standards

### Minimum Requirements

**Color Contrast**: WCAG AA (4.5:1 for text, 3:1 for large text)
**Alt Text**: Required for all images
**Keyboard Navigation**: Full functionality without mouse
**Screen Readers**: Semantic HTML, ARIA labels
**Captions**: Required for all video/audio content

### Inclusive Design Practices

- Design for cognitive diversity (clear language, chunked content)
- Support multiple learning styles (text, audio, visual)
- Mobile-first responsive design
- Low-bandwidth considerations
- Dark mode option for light sensitivity
